Testing Method: What Makes Jewelry Good?

We didn't just wear these necklaces—we subjected them to rigorous stress tests. Our evaluation focused on materials, construction quality, and most importantly, clasp strength. After all, if the clasp fails, the jewelry becomes useless.

Here are the main criteria we used:

  • Material Check: Is it made from 925 Sterling Silver or 316L Stainless Steel? If the seller doesn't specify one of these materials, assume it's cheap brass that will tarnish within a week. Cheap plating poses a significant risk.
  • Clasp Durability: We tested how easily the clasp pin bent or broke when given a quick pull.
  • Weight and Comfort: The Modian necklace is designed for all-day wear. Heavy jewelry tends to become uncomfortable.
  • Warranty and Service: Does the company provide support if something goes wrong? This is particularly important for fine jewelry purchases.

Verdict: Always verify the materials first. If the description simply says "Alloy," steer clear. Look specifically for 925 Sterling Silver.
