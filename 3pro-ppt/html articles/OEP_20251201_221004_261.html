Week 1: Getting Used to Them

I wore the hat for short periods at first. I needed to see if the crown (the top part) was too shallow or too deep. Some hats sit too high on your head. They look silly. Other hats pull down too low and cover your ears.

Early Experience and Adjustments

Breaking it In

I decided to break it in slowly. I followed these steps:

  1. Wear the cap daily for two hours.
  2. Slightly curve the brim tighter each night.
  3. Mist the front panel lightly with water to encourage softening.

By day seven, the cap was starting to mold to my head shape. The slight discomfort was gone. It felt like my own hat already.

Verdict: The fit is excellent for most head sizes. Be patient; the cotton needs about a week to soften up.
